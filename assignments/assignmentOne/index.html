Guitar Maintenance

Guitars are a pretty tricky thing to deal with, and sometimes
they can be a real pain to work with.

List of Materials

Required

  • Guitar Strings
  • Wire Cutters (or something to cut the strings)

Recommended

  • Microfiber Cloth
  • Lemon Oil

Convenience

  • String Winder

String Replacement

You need to change strings every so often so that they feel
comfortable playing and aren't rusty.

To replace guitar strings, unwind them from the headstock of the guitar
and take them out of the tuning pegs. Then cut off the tops of the strings
and pull what's left of the strings through the back of the guitar.

Then, get your new strings and put them through the back of the guitar
so that they are all the way pulled through to the other side.
Put the new strings through the tuning pegs on the headstock but leave
2 to 3 inches of slack from the peg. This will allow you to have multiple
winds around the tuning peg allowing for better tuning stability.

Finally, bend the top of the string on the side past the tuning peg
to keep it from coming out. You can then start twisting the tuning
knob in the direction that the string winds toward the inside,
putting less un-needed stress on the strings.

Neck Cleaning

To clean the neck of a guitar, make sure the strings are off.
This will make the cleaning a lot easier.

First, put a few drops of lemon oil in some spots along the fretboard
and then rub it in lightly with a microfiber cloth. This treats the
so that it looks nice, feels nice, and is healthy.

If there is any real gunk stuck on the fretboard, you can apply some
pressure with the microfiber cloth with lemon oil to try and get it off.
This will make your guitar look and feel nice to play!
